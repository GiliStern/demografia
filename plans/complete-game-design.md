Game Design Document: Demographia

1.0 Vision Statement & Core Pillars

This document serves as the singular creative and strategic vision for the project codenamed "Demographia." It is the foundational blueprint that defines the game's core experience, mechanics, and artistic direction. Every decision, from asset creation to system design, should align with the principles outlined herein to ensure the delivery of a cohesive, compelling, and deeply satirical gameplay experience.

Vision Statement

Demographia is a comedic, satirical survival game that uses the addictive, accessible "Vampire Survivors" gameplay loop as a vehicle for sharp cultural commentary on modern Israeli society. Players will navigate a chaotic, exaggerated version of Israel, surviving endless waves of humorous stereotypes and cultural phenomena, transforming the mundane into the menacing for a uniquely hilarious and replayable experience.

Core Pillars

Our design philosophy is guided by three fundamental pillars that inform every aspect of the game's development.

- Satirical Chaos The core gameplay loop of surviving against ever-growing, endless hordes is the perfect mechanical metaphor for the delightful, overwhelming chaos of daily life in Israel. Every enemy is a caricature drawn from a recognizable facet of Israeli society—from minor annoyances to major political phenomena. This creates a constant stream of "in-jokes" and social commentary, turning the player's struggle for survival into a running gag about the absurdities of the culture.
- Accessible Gameplay, Deep References By intentionally mirroring the well-established mechanics of Vampire Survivors, we make the game instantly intuitive and playable. This accessibility is a strategic choice; it removes barriers to entry and allows the player to immediately focus on the game's true purpose: the humor and cultural references. The depth comes not from complex controls, but from recognizing and appreciating the layers of satire embedded in every weapon, enemy, and upgrade.
- Unapologetically Israeli Every element of Demographia is steeped in Israeli culture. The protagonist is a national icon, the weapons are everyday objects, the enemies are social archetypes, the meta-upgrades are political puns, and the soundtrack is infused with local musical motifs. The user interface and all in-game text are in Hebrew, creating total immersion in this hyper-real, comedic reflection of Israel. This is not a game that gestures at a theme; it is a game that lives and breathes it.

With this vision established, we will now explore the world that serves as the stage for this satire.

---

2.0 The World: A Satirical Mirror of Israel

The stages in Demographia are more than just backdrops; they are active participants in the game's satire. Each level is an infinite-scrolling caricature of a key location, representing a distinct and often conflicting facet of the Israeli cultural landscape. The environment itself, from the props to the ambient sounds, reinforces the central comedic themes.

2.1 Stage 1: Tel Aviv Streets (רחובות תל אביב)

Thematic Overview: This stage represents the secular, chaotic, and relentlessly modern heart of Israel. It is a world of non-stop construction, relentless hustle, and a vibrant, sometimes absurd, cultural scene. The player's survival here is a battle against the overwhelming tide of urban trends, bureaucratic nightmares, and geopolitical anxieties.

Visual & Audio Direction

- Landmarks: Pixelated renditions of the Dizengoff fountain and Azrieli Towers will scroll by in the background.
- Props & Tilesets: The ground is a mix of asphalt, bike lanes, and torn-up pavement. The play area is littered with props like Tel-Ofan bikes, electric scooters, falafel stands, and construction cones.
- Ambiance: The soundscape is a chiptune blend of car horns, distant sirens, stray cats, and the faint beat of electronic music, punctuated by the tel_aviv_theme.wav track.

Enemy Roster Analysis

Enemy Satirical Representation
חתולי רחוב (Street Cats) Replaces "bats" as the most basic, ubiquitous, and swarming mob. A nod to Tel Aviv's massive and beloved stray cat population.
היפסטר (Hipster) A common enemy that embodies the trendy, non-conformist culture prevalent in the city, likely attacking with vinyl records or cold brew coffee.
תייר (Tourist) A slow, meandering enemy that moves unpredictably, blocking paths and representing the constant, sometimes disorienting, flow of tourism.
נחיל קורקינטים (Swarm of Scooters) A fast-moving swarm of electric scooters, satirizing their chaotic and often dangerous proliferation on city sidewalks.
טיקטוקיסט (TikToker) An enemy that stops to perform a dance, creating a distracting area-of-effect attack that represents the modern plague of public content creation.
עבודות הרכבת הקלה (Subway Construction) A slow-moving but durable miniboss event that blocks paths, satirizing the city's seemingly endless and disruptive light rail construction.
מחאת קפלן (Kaplan Protests) A chaotic miniboss event where a massive, surging crowd fills the screen, representing the large-scale political protests that define the modern era.
איראן (Iran) The stage's final boss. A comically exaggerated representation of the ultimate external threat, turning a geopolitical reality into a video game villain.

2.2 Stage 2: Old Jerusalem (ירושלים העתיקה)

Thematic Overview: This stage is a satirical nexus of ancient history, profound religion, and perpetual conflict. The atmosphere is heavier, steeped in millennia of tradition and tension. Survival here is a journey through a dense tapestry of faith, history, and bureaucracy, where ancient ghosts walk alongside modern-day functionaries.

Visual & Audio Direction

- Landmarks: The background will feature iconic silhouettes like the Western Wall and the Dome of the Rock, presented in a neutral, stylized manner.
- Props & Tilesets: The ground is made of ancient stone alleyways, archways, and mosaic floors. Props include spice stalls, menorahs, clay water jars, and olive baskets.
- Ambiance: The jerusalem_theme.wav track is accompanied by a mix of distant prayer chants, church bells, and the occasional shofar blast, creating a layered and atmospheric soundscape.

Enemy Roster Analysis

Enemy Satirical Representation
עולי רגל (Pilgrims) A common enemy type that moves in dense, determined groups, representing the unending flow of religious pilgrims from all faiths.
שומרי סדר (Guards) Basic aggressive mobs that represent the heavy security and order-keeping presence throughout the ancient city.
רוחות בירוקרטיה (Spirits of Bureaucracy) Intangible, ghost-like enemies that slow the player, satirizing the maddening and invisible bureaucratic hurdles of the city's religious authorities.
צלבנים חמושים (Armed Crusaders) A historical miniboss, representing one of the many foreign conquests that have defined the city's bloody history.
לגיונרים רומאים (Roman Legionaries) Another historical miniboss, an echo of the Roman empire's foundational impact and destruction in Jerusalem.
מפקד ממלוכי (Mamluk Commander) A powerful miniboss representing the Mamluk Sultanate's historical rule over the city.
נבוכדנצר (Nebuchadnezzar) The first major boss. A historical figure responsible for the First Temple's destruction, now a pixelated tyrant.
טיטוס (Titus) The second major boss. The Roman general responsible for the Second Temple's destruction, framed as a climactic historical antagonist.

Having defined the stages of our satire, let us now turn to the protagonist who will navigate them and the cultural arsenal they will wield.

3.0 Player Character & Arsenal

The player's identity and the tools at their disposal are central to the game's satire. The protagonist is not a generic hero, but a symbol of national identity. The weapons are not swords and spells, but mundane objects of Israeli life, elevated to instruments of chaotic defense.

The Protagonist: שרוליק (Shrulik)

The sole starting character is Shrulik, the legendary Israeli caricature created by Dosh. He is depicted in his iconic "tembel" hat and sandals. This choice immediately grounds the game in a specific brand of nostalgic Zionism and sets a lighthearted, self-referential tone. Shrulik represents a classic, perhaps naive, ideal of the Israeli spirit, making his battle against the absurdities of modern society all the more poignant and humorous.

His starting weapon is the צבר (Prickly Pear / Sabra), a fruit that is thorny on the outside but sweet on the inside—the very symbol used to describe native-born Israelis.

Weapons of Mass Culture: The Arsenal

The initial weapon set is a collection of mundane items transformed into powerful, screen-clearing armaments. Each weapon and its evolution serves as a cultural gag.

Weapon Name (Hebrew) Gameplay Function (Replaces) Visual Description Cultural & Satirical Significance
צבר (Sabra) Magic Wand Fires prickly pear fruits at the nearest enemy. The quintessential symbol of the native-born Israeli. Using it as a basic projectile is a foundational joke.
Evolved: קקטוס קדוש (Holy Cactus) Holy Wand Fires a continuous, rapid stream of prickly pears. The evolution name absurdly imbues the national symbol with religious, "holy" power.
כסאות כתר (Keter Chairs) Runetracer Bounces white plastic chairs around the screen, piercing enemies. The Keter plastic chair is a ubiquitous, cheap, and indestructible icon of Israeli gardens, balconies, and cafes.
Evolved: אין עתיד (No Future) NO FUTURE The chairs multiply and bounce faster, creating a chaotic visual storm. A direct, cynical joke referencing the punk slogan, implying a future filled with nothing but bouncing plastic chairs.
כפרות (Kaparot) King Bible Chickens circle the player, damaging nearby enemies. A direct reference to the pre-Yom Kippur ritual where a chicken is swung over one's head to atone for sins.
Evolved: סליחות לא קדושות (Unholy Vespers) Unholy Vespers The chickens circle continuously and deal more damage. The name is a direct, humorous inversion of the original "Unholy Vespers" and a play on the Selichot prayers.
פיתות (Pitas) Axe Arcs of pita bread are lobbed upwards, dealing area damage. The most basic staple food, now weaponized. A perfect example of turning the mundane into the mighty.
Evolved: בורקס המוות (Burekas of Death) Death Spiral Pitas are replaced by scythe-like burekas that fly out in all directions. A comedic escalation from one common baked good to another, with a menacing, over-the-top name.
מגן דוד (Star of David) Knife Fires a sharp Star of David in the direction the player is facing. The ultimate national and religious symbol, reduced to a simple, fast-firing video game projectile.
Evolved: אלף חיתוכים (Thousand Edge) Thousand Edge Fires an endless stream of Stars of David with no delay. The name is a literal translation, creating a dark but humorous image of being defeated by a "thousand cuts" from a national symbol.

Passive Items & Pickups: The Israeli Condition

Passive items and temporary floor pickups continue the satire by turning abstract social concepts and everyday items into tangible gameplay bonuses.

- פריווילגיה (Privilege) -> Replaces Hollow Heart (Max Health): The abstract concept of societal privilege is satirically quantified as a direct increase in the player's health pool, making them more resilient.
- ווסאח (Wassach) -> Replaces Empty Tome (Cooldown Reduction): This slang term, roughly meaning "swagger" or unearned confidence, directly translates to the ability to use weapons more frequently.
- פרוטקשן (Protection) -> Replaces Armor: A common slang term for protection money paid to organized crime. Here, it ironically provides legitimate damage reduction. This item can be found in-run and also purchased as a permanent upgrade in the meta-shop, reflecting the cynical idea that protection is both a temporary and a long-term 'investment'.
- גת (Gat) -> Replaces Spinach (Damage Up): A stimulant leaf chewed in certain communities is satirically linked to increasing one's power and damage output.
- כנפי השכינה (Wings of the Divine Presence) -> Replaces Wings (Movement Speed): A joke that takes a sublime, mystical religious concept and reduces it to a common video game "speed boost."
- מרבין בשמחה (Increase the Joy) -> Replaces Duplicator (Amount): This well-known phrase, associated with the holiday of Purim, is comedically re-purposed to mean firing more projectiles.
- חמין (Hamin) -> Replaces Floor Chicken (Heals HP): The iconic slow-cooked Shabbat stew now serves as the game's primary health recovery item, dropped by defeated enemies.

These items transform the act of collecting power-ups into a continuous engagement with the game's cultural commentary, which extends even further into the permanent progression system.

4.0 Meta-Progression & Long-Term Engagement

The meta-shop is designed to extend the game's satire beyond a single 30-minute run. It reframes the concept of permanent upgrades as a commentary on Israeli political, social, and economic values. Players spend the מטבעות זהב (Gold Coins) they've collected to invest in power-ups whose names are loaded with cultural and political subtext.

The Meta-Shop: Power-Ups as Political Commentary

The shop allows players to permanently enhance their stats, with each upgrade representing a distinct piece of social satire.

Upgrade (Hebrew Name) Satirical Analysis

פרוטקשן (Protection) -> Armor Uses the common slang for extortion money to represent damage reduction, a cynical joke about how "protection" is acquired.
פריווילגיה (Privilege) -> Max Health Makes the literal connection between societal privilege and greater resilience or survivability.
מד"א (Magen David Adom) -> Recovery Named after Israel's national emergency medical service, directly linking health recovery to a real-world organization.
ווסאח (Wassach) -> Cooldown The slang for "swagger" is what allows the player to act more frequently, a commentary on the power of unearned confidence.
נרות שבת (Shabbat Candles) -> Area The holy light of Shabbat candles is mechanically represented as an increase to a weapon's area of effect.
כנפי השכינה (Wings of the Divine Presence) -> Speed A sublime religious concept is commodified into a permanent movement speed upgrade.
התמדה (Persistence) -> Duration A wholesome personal virtue, now a purchasable stat that makes weapon effects last longer.
מרבין בשמחה (Increase the Joy) -> Amount The festive Purim commandment is quantified as the ability to permanently fire more projectiles.

הפרשת חלה (Separating Challah) -> Luck This references a Jewish ritual believed to bring blessings. In the game, it is humorously quantified as a direct increase in the "luck" stat.

תעוות בצע (Greed) -> Greed A literal translation that directly names the sin associated with earning more money in the game.
עין הרע (Evil Eye) -> Curse A common superstition. Here, it mechanically increases game difficulty, perfectly aligning the cultural concept with its effect.
אופציות (Options) -> Reroll A term from the worlds of high-tech and finance, satirically used to mean getting another chance at a level-up choice.

חותם (Seal) -> Seal The ability to permanently banish an item from all future runs, framed with a term implying finality and authority.

Unlocks & Achievements

The system for unlocking new characters, stages, Arcanas, and other game features will follow the established Vampire Survivors model. However, the specific goals required for these unlocks will be thematically tied to the Israeli experience. For example, an achievement might be titled "Survived a trip to Misrad Hapnim" or "Found parking in Tel Aviv," tying gameplay milestones to relatable cultural struggles.

With the core mechanics and progression systems defined, we turn to the aesthetic that will bring this satirical world to life.

5.0 Artistic & Audio Direction

The overall aesthetic of Demographia must be a deliberate and careful blend of retro gaming nostalgia and sharp, modern satire. The presentation should feel charming, chaotic, and unmistakably Israeli, using its audio-visual language to support the comedic tone at every turn.

Visual Philosophy: Modern Satire in Retro Pixels

- Core Art Style: The game will utilize a Pixel retro aesthetic. The base resolution for characters, environmental tiles, and weapons will be 32x32 pixels. This style evokes a sense of nostalgia while providing the necessary visual abstraction to make the satirical caricatures charming rather than grotesque.
- Color Palette: The palette must be bright, humorous, and comedic. We will avoid dark, gritty, or overly realistic colors. The goal is to reinforce the lighthearted and satirical tone, ensuring the game feels like a parody, not a grim survival horror.
- Animation: Animations will be kept simple, using 2-4 frame loops for most movements and effects. This maintains the retro feel, ensures visual clarity when hundreds of enemies are on screen, and keeps the production scope manageable.

Audio Landscape: The Sounds of Israel

The audio will be as culturally specific as the visuals, creating an immersive and memorable soundscape.

- Music: The Original Soundtrack (OST) will be composed in a chiptune style but will heavily incorporate melodies and motifs from Israeli folk and jazz traditions. Each stage will have its own distinct theme, such as music/tel_aviv_theme.wav and music/jerusalem_theme.wav, to establish a unique atmosphere.
- Sound Effects (SFX): All sound effects—from weapon impacts to collecting pickups—will be satisfying and styled to fit the retro aesthetic. Special attention will be paid to key thematic moments; for instance, major boss spawns will be announced with short, distinct Hebrew voice lines to heighten the immersion and humor. The sound of a chest opening (sfx/chest_open.wav) should be an exciting and rewarding audio cue.

Finally, we will examine how the player interacts with these systems through a carefully themed user interface.

---

6.0 User Interface (UI) & Experience (UX) Flow

The UI/UX philosophy for Demographia is straightforward: provide a seamless, intuitive user journey by mirroring the proven structure of Vampire Survivors, while completely reskinning every visual element and text label to fit the Israeli theme. This ensures immediate familiarity for genre veterans and newcomers alike, allowing them to navigate the game effortlessly. All text throughout the game will be in Hebrew to maintain full thematic immersion.

Main Menu

- Visuals: A static background image of a pixel-art Tel Aviv street (bg/tel_aviv_loop.png) with the game's title prominently displayed.
- Functionality: A vertical stack of primary navigation buttons.
  - שחק (Play): Initiates the gameplay loop, leading to Character Selection.
  - חנות שדרוגים (Meta-Shop): Opens the permanent upgrade screen.
  - הישגים (Achievements): Displays the list of unlocked and available achievements.
  - הגדרות (Settings): Opens the settings menu for audio and other options.
  - צא (Exit): Closes the application (for desktop builds).

Character Selection Screen

- Visuals: A grid-based layout (bg/char_select.png) displaying portraits (sprites/portraits_64x64.png) of all available characters. Initially, only שרוליק is selectable, with others shown as locked.
- Functionality:
  - Displays the character's name, starting weapon, and passive ability.
  - בחר דמות (Select Character) button to confirm selection.
  - חזור (Back) button to return to the Main Menu.

Stage Selection Screen

- Visuals: A horizontal carousel (bg/stage_select.png) featuring preview images (sprites/stage_previews_256x144.png) for each unlocked stage.
- Functionality:
  - Displays the stage name (e.g., רחובות תל אביב, ירושלים העתיקה) and a brief thematic description.
  - בחר שלב (Select Stage) button to start the game.
  - חזור (Back) button to return to Character Selection.

Meta-Shop Screen

- Visuals: A list-based interface (bg/shop.png) showing all permanent power-ups.
- Functionality:
  - Each entry displays the upgrade's icon, name, current level, and cost in מטבעות זהב.
  - A קנה (Buy) button allows the player to purchase an upgrade level.
  - The player's current gold total is always visible.
  - A חזור (Back) button returns to the Main Menu.

In-Game HUD

The in-game Heads-Up Display provides critical information without cluttering the screen. All numerical counters will remain in standard Left-to-Right (LTR) format for clarity, while all text labels will be in Hebrew.

- Top Center: A run timer (mm:ss).
- Top Left: Player level (מספר רמה) and an XP bar.
- Top Right: Current gold collected (מטבעות זהב).
- Left Side: A vertical list of icons representing the player's current weapons and their levels.
- Right Side: A vertical list of icons representing the player's current passive items.
